Speed

  • Speed = s
  • Time = t
  • Distance = d

Speed is measured in km/h (Kilometers per hour) or m/s (meters per second)

Time is measured in s (seconds) or h (hours)

Distance is measured in km (kilometers) or m (meters)

Hover over button for answers

  1. Dougs bike moves 30m in 15 seconds, what is his speed?
  2. Margies car travels 800km in 10 hours. What is her speed?
  3. Poppy walks to work in 30 minutes, she walks an average speed of 2.0m/s. How far away is her work in km?

Acceleration

  • Acceleration = a
  • Final speed = vf
  • Initial speed = vi
  • Final time = tf
  • Initial time = ti

Acceleration is measured in m/s² (meters per second squared)

Time is measured in s (seconds)

Speed is measured in m/s (meters per second)

Acceleration Practice Questions

Hover over button for answers

  1. A lizard accelerates from 2 m/s to 10 m/s in 4 seconds. What is the lizard's average acceleration?
  2. A ball is dropped from the top of a building. After 2 seconds, it’s velocity is measured to be 19.6 m/s. What is the acceleration for the dropped ball?
  3. If a Ferrari, with an initial velocity of 10 m/s, accelerates at a rate of 50 m/s for 3 seconds, what will its final velocity be?

Force

  • Force = F
  • Mass = m
  • Acceleration = a

Force is measured in N (newtons)

Mass is measured in kg (kilograms)

Acceleration is measured inm/s² (meters per second squared)

Force Practice Questions

Hover over button for answers

  1. What is the acceleration of a 50 kg object pushed with a force of 500 newtons?
  2. A 50.0 kg skater pushed by a friend accelerates 5.0 m/s².How much force did the friend apply?
  3. If a 60 kg person on a 15 kg sled is pushed with a force of 300 N, what will be their acceleration?

Work

  • Work = W
  • Force = F
  • Distance = d

Work is measured in J (joules/newton meters)

Force is measured in N (newtons)

Distance is measured in m (meters)

Work can also be represented by the formula is "W=mad", with "m" meaning mass, "a" meaning acceleration, and "d" meaning distance.

  2. Joe mows a schoolyard for 100m straight, he used 1000J of force. What was his work?
  3. A very dedicated man pushes a box 2.0 km, he uses 6000J of force. What is his work?
